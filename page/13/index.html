MySQL high availability comparison: service versus data

Fri, Mar 2, 2012 When people ask me about high availability, I often suggest that it’s helpful to understand whether you’re most interested in service availability, data availability, or both. Of course, you want both – but if cost is an object, you may end up relaxing your requirements. The typical example of an application that needs service availability but doesn’t have strong data availability requirements is something that’s ad-supported. Some pt-table-checksum FAQs

Thu, Feb 9, 2012 After the recent update to pt-table-checksum, I’ve seen a few FAQs about it. Q: is it still multi-threaded/parallel? A: No, that was a pile of bugs and complexity. If you need to run the tool in parallel to take advantage of powerful hardware, you can run several instances, say, one per database. Q: what chunk size should I use?
